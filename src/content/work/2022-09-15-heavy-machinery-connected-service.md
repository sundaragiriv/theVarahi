## The problem worth solving

Heavy-machinery and long-lifecycle capital assets — surface and underground mining equipment worth millions per unit — live or die on uptime. Field service engineering workflows were fragmented across regional divisions, equipment faults were caught late, and the wear-and-tear technicians found on site rarely turned into structured, priced service.

## What was built

As lead domain architect, Venkata standardised global field-service workflows and architected an integrated multi-tenant CX ecosystem linking **SAP C4C, FSM and CPQ for Service** into a legacy SAP ECC core — with foundational blueprints and design standards synchronising multi-million-dollar equipment master records and installed bases.

**SAP IoT was integrated with Service Cloud** so real-time equipment monitoring could proactively trigger service tickets and improve asset uptime; ticket workflows aligned to those IoT alerts **cut customer service response time by 30%**. A deficiency-driven monetisation model let technicians capture machinery wear and structural deficiencies on site, converting unpredictable field findings into structured, high-margin service contracts and automated repair quotes. Fit-to-Standard governance retired custom code by mapping component-exchange logistics and Resource-Related Billing to native platform frameworks, and the FSM scheduling optimiser was tuned to mining safety clearances so no technician was dispatched without certification.

## Why it matters

This is the Cognition layer earning its keep — IoT and predictive routing embedded where they pay back, on assets where downtime is measured in six figures a day.
