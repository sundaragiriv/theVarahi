## The problem worth solving

Enterprise clients across multiple industries needed to unify sales, service and field operations on a single, governed SAP foundation — re-platforming legacy CRM and service systems onto SAP's V2 CX stack without recreating the integration debt that had made the old estate brittle.

## What was built

Leading the CX practice's architecture, Venkata delivered end-to-end implementations spanning **lead-to-cash, service-to-cash and field-to-finish**. Sales Cloud V2 was configured for advanced opportunity management, guided selling and approval orchestration integrated with SAP CPQ and S/4HANA pricing. Service Cloud V2 handled omnichannel ticketing, SLA tracking and IoT-based service triggers wired into FSM and S/4 service orders. FSM covered dispatching, resource planning and mobile technician execution with offline closure.

At the centre sat a **BTP/CPI integration hub** governing master-data replication, transactional synchronisation and event-driven APIs between the CX clouds and S/4HANA — with custom iFlows for real-time updates of business partners, service contracts and installed-base objects. AI-assisted analytics and predictive routing used SAP Joule and SAC Predictive Insights to sharpen lead conversion and SLA adherence.

## The outcome

A fully integrated CX + S/4 architecture that reduced **quote-to-order cycle time by 35%** and **service-order processing by 40%**, enabled predictive dispatching that lifted **first-time-fix by 28%**, and achieved seamless real-time synchronisation of **500k+ master records** over BTP CPI. The reusable CX Integration Blueprint from this work became a standard for subsequent V2 deployments.

## Why it matters

This is the three layers as one design problem — platform, cognition and human on a single governed architecture — with the numbers to show it lands.
