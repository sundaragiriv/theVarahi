## The problem worth solving

The world's largest maker of AC drives, servo/motion control and industrial robots carried a large global installed base with a lucrative aftermarket — and service delivery models that had diverged across disparate global divisions, each carrying its own legacy customisation and technical debt.

## What was built

Acting as the primary functional authority, Venkata led the strategic blueprinting and execution of a global SAP service and CRM transformation, harmonising complex service delivery for high-precision heavy robotics onto **one model** across divisions. The lever was **Fit-to-Standard**: intricate industrial service workflows were mapped directly to native SAP Service Cloud V2 and FSM capabilities, eliminating **over 40% of legacy custom code** and the technical debt that came with it.

The end-to-end **Request-to-Service** lifecycle was architected as a seamless pipeline across SAP BTP, CPI, S/4HANA Service and FSM — a unified model for automated case handling, resource scheduling and asset-entitlement verification. Service monetisation was designed in properly: Resource-Related Billing (RRB), complex time-and-expense logging, and field-service parts returns, so heavy component replacements and multi-tier warranty claims synchronised cleanly with S/4HANA Finance and Billing.

## Why it matters

Removing 40% of the custom code is not housekeeping — it is what makes the estate AI-ready and cheap to run. Fit-to-Standard is the discipline behind Varahi's Platform layer, and this is the proof it scales to the hardest global installed bases.
