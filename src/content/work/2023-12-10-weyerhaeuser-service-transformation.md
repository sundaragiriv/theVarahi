## The problem worth solving

One of the world's largest forest-products and timberland companies needed to move sales and service off aging legacy commercial systems and onto SAP's cloud CX stack — across five distinct heavy-industrial business lines, five core value streams and 25 highly distinct end-to-end processes, for more than 600 users.

## What was built

As OTC track lead, Venkata commanded a cross-functional onshore/offshore team of **27 consultants** through the SAP **C4C Sales & Service V2** implementation. Legacy commercial systems were decommissioned and their critical sales and service functions rebuilt as cloud-native applications on **SAP BTP and Azure App Pass**, integrated with S/4HANA, SAP MDG, PriceFX and SAP Qualtrics over CPI middleware.

The hardest work was in the supply-chain seam: multi-tier Available-to-Promise (ATP) functions were re-engineered directly into the customer-experience layer, aligning front-end service demand, component return orders and credit/debit-memo processes with real-time mill production planning and inventory consumption. Scoping, hands-on C4C configuration, iFlow activation in CPI, and mentoring the technical team through the most complex lumber-business scenarios all sat with one architect.

## Why it matters

Scale is its own test. Holding five business lines and 600 users to a single design — while replacing the legacy estate underneath them — is exactly the "one team, one backlog" discipline the practice is built on.
