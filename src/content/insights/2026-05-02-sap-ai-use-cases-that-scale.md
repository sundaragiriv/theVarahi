Most SAP + AI pilots fail at scale because they depend on fragile data assumptions or undefined ownership.

Use cases that scale tend to share the same traits: clear operator, clear data boundary, and clear success metric.

## Five durable patterns

1. Service triage assistance with human final disposition
2. Knowledge-grounded resolution suggestions for support teams
3. Planner exception summaries with linked source transactions
4. Document intake and classification with confidence thresholds
5. Cross-system case handoff recommendations with audit trail

## What these patterns have in common

- A measurable time or quality outcome
- A defined handoff to a human or system of record
- Policy and logging built into the workflow

The question is not "Can AI do this task?" The right question is "Can this workflow run safely and repeatedly inside our operating model?"

If the answer is yes, scaling becomes execution discipline, not experimental luck.
