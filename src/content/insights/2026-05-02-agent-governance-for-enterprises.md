Agent pilots fail less because of model quality and more because ownership is unclear. When incidents happen, teams cannot answer who approved behavior, what policy applied, or why a decision was taken.

You need a governance model that is small, enforceable, and visible.

## Minimum control stack

- Identity: explicit service and user context for every action
- Policy: machine-readable constraints that can be audited
- Trace: immutable event logs for prompts, tools, and outcomes
- Review: escalation path for high-impact decisions

## Keep it lightweight

Do not build committee-heavy governance. Build policy-backed automation.

- Pre-approve low-risk action classes
- Require human disposition for financial or safety impact
- Automatically capture rationale and evidence for each action

Governance should be a deployment accelerator, not a blocker. If your controls are codified, you can move quickly without losing accountability.
